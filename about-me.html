about me

    My passion for bioinformatics sparked while I majored in Cellular Molecular Biology. Here, I took a course in the Introduction of Bioinformatics and discovered my knack for computational methods over experimental techniques. My mentor, Dr. Luis Vazquez-QuiÃ±ones, invited me to a summer internship where I combined multiple bioinformatic tools to identify a unique motif that could potentially be incorporated into a biosensor for cancer detection. This experience inspired me to pursue a career in connecting biologist with the necessary tools to elevate their benchmark projects.

    After this experience, I was advised to take a Python course, but initially dismissed the idea. However, I would come to regret that choice as I struggled through an undergradiate python course during my master's degree. Throughout my master's program, I acquired skills that were not covered in my undergraduate training including bash, C++, and a myriad of next-generation sequencing programs. Culminating my master's, I realized how easily biologists could learn these skills and apply them to their everyday analyses.

    Currently, I am a candidate for the Bioinformatics and Genomics doctoral program at Penn State University utilizing sketching approaches to measure selection pressures for metagenomics in the Koslicki lab. Reflecting on my journey, I see how I was able to transition from an experimentalist in training to developing tools and applying computational models. As I approach my thesis defense, my goal is to continue my pursuit in expanding biological knowledge through collaborations utilizing bioinformatic and computational skills, and give back in the form of workshops to the experimental community who first shaped me.
